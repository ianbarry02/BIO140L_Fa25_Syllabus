Foundations of Biology I Laboratory (BIO140L), Fall 2025

Course Expectations

Course Description: Students learn the process of science and the basics of experimental design while building scientific writing skills. Students learn foundational techniques of molecular biology (pipetting, DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis and basic bioinformatics) in the context of a DNA Barcoding project. Students will leave this course with the preparation needed to design their own independent DNA barcoding projects. May be used for general education credit. Prerequisite(s): BIO140. Corequisite(s): BIO140.

 

Upon successful completion of BIO140L we will be able to:

●        Understand and apply the process of science

●        Interpret phylogenetic trees

●        Use basic molecular biology techniques like pipetting, DNA extraction, PCR, gels and DNA sequencing

●        Practice scientific communication and collaboration

●        Design and conduct an independent DNA barcoding project in BIO 150 Lab

●        Demonstrate basic skills commonly expected in molecular biology research and internship opportunities

●        See ourselves as scientists

For many activities/assignments, you will work in a group with the other students at your lab bench. For group assignments, each member of the group must contribute equally for each to receive full points. If problems arise due to unequal effort, any member of the group may call a meeting with the lab instructor to discuss the problem.

 

Prelab quizzes are typically due 2 hours before the start of each lab. The lowest Prelab quiz grade will be dropped for each student; please note, this does not include the Phylogeny quiz or Checkpoint quizzes.

 

The Phylogeny quiz, the two Checkpoint quizzes and the Lab Final exam will be taken in class, individually, with no outside resources. Any missed quizzes including the Phylogeny and Checkpoint quizzes must be made up within 7 days, or a zero will be entered into the gradebook.

 

If a student wishes to appeal an assignment or an exam grade, they must present their case to me within a week of receiving the graded material. After a week, no appeals will be accepted regardless of their merit.

 

Peer evaluations/Lab Preparedness:

This 5% of your lab grade includes a midterm and a final peer evaluation, where group members evaluate each other. To be a good group member, you must come to the lab prepared and actively participate in completing all assignments in and out of the lab.

Disruptive Events:

Laboratory Attendance:

On-time attendance in lab is required. All the labs will be carried out in small groups, so missing a lab will put you AND your group members at a serious disadvantage.

 

Respiratory and Intestinal Viruses including Norovirus and Covid:

DO NOT come to lab if you are vomiting/have diarrhea and/or have a fever. You should be fever-free and/or intestinal symptom-free for 24 hours before returning to class. Please consider wearing a mask if you have an active cough. Contact your instructor right away and explain the circumstances, and they will work with you to make up any missed work.

 

What do I do If I miss a Lab? If you must miss a lab due to a planned, verifiable reason, it must be cleared by your lab instructor in advance, and you must make arrangements to work with your lab instructor on completing the missed material. Students are expected to make up any missed assignments or assessments whether the absence is excused or unexcused.  Please note that, as specified in JMU Academic Affairs Policy #16, accommodations will be made for court-imposed legal obligations, religious observances, active military service, and official University functions. In all cases documentation must be provided to the instructor within one week of the missed lab. If you are ill, please contact the instructor as soon as possible to devise a plan for making up the missed lab. It is not necessary that the instructor be informed of the nature of the illness or emergency.

 

Please email your instructor if you are experiencing extenuating circumstances. If you miss too much class time due to unexpected health-related or other circumstances, an incomplete or class withdrawal option can be considered depending on the situation. See Adding/Dropping Classes above for additional options. For extended absences, students are urged to contact the Office of the Dean of Students.

 

Absences not approved by your instructor are considered unexcused. The first unexcused lab absence will result in a zero for the quiz and any other items due that day. A second unexcused absence will result in a 10% deduction from the semester lab course grade. A third unexcused absence will result in a zero for the semester lab course grade. Tardiness to lab is also not acceptable. If you are 20 minutes late to lab on three days, that will count as one unexcused absence.
